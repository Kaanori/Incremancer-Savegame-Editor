Trading Strategy — STARGATE SK v3

⚠️ WARNING: This strategy combines the STARGATE risk framework with the SK-System (Sequence Structure + Fibonacci Retracement + Fibonacci Extension + Liquidation Heatmap + Volume Profile Confluence), but in a simplified and more executable format. It is not independently audited and must be forward-tested before live deployment. No system has a 100% hit rate — this system works with probabilities, confluence, and strict risk control.

## Core Philosophy

- Markets move in waves: impulse → correction → impulse — in both directions
- Always analyze top-down: Weekly → Daily → 4H → 1H
- Never draw a sequence before activation — only after the A-Point is confirmed broken
- Confluence matters, but only a small number of factors should drive execution
- The strategy trades specific levels, not vague zones
- “No setup” is a valid and correct decision
- The goal is not to catch every move, but to catch high-quality moves with controlled risk

## Rules

1. Only trade BTC — no altcoins unless the strategy is explicitly expanded
2. Max leverage: 3x Long / 2x Short — no exceptions
3. Max risk per trade: 1.5% of capital for standard setups / 2.0% for A+ setups — if the stop loss would lose more, reduce position size
4. Max open positions: 2 — one per direction max, only one short position at a time
5. Max daily drawdown: 6% — stop trading for the day if hit
6. Max total drawdown: 12% — reduce all position sizes by 50%
7. Max total drawdown: 20% — A+ setups only, no shorts
8. Max total drawdown: 30% — stop all trading immediately and review the strategy
9. After 3 consecutive losses — pause new entries for 24 hours
10. Never increase size to recover losses — never average down
11. Never trade against the Weekly regime — no exceptions
12. Minimum CRV (risk-reward ratio): 3:1 — do not enter if the CRV is below 3:1

## SK-System — Fibonacci Tool Settings

### Fibonacci Retracement (Entry Zone Identification)

Use the following retracement levels:

- 50.0% — primary entry
- 55.9% — secondary entry
- 61.8% — golden ratio entry
- 66.7% — final zone entry
- 78.6% — stop-loss reference

The active entry zone spans from 50.0% to 66.7%.

### Fibonacci Extension (Target Identification)

Use the following extension levels:

- 161.8% — TP2 minimum sequence target
- 180.9% — intermediate SK target
- 200.0% — final sequence target

The active target zone spans from 161.8% to 200.0%.

## Sequence Logic

### Bullish Sequence

1. Zero Point = swing low
2. Impulse up creates the A-Point
3. Correction down creates the B-Point — must hold above Zero Point
4. Sequence activates only when price breaks above the A-Point
5. The TCL Buy Zone is measured with Fibonacci Retracement from Zero to A
6. The extension target is projected with Fibonacci Extension from Zero → A → B

### Bearish Sequence

1. Zero Point = swing high
2. Impulse down creates the A-Point
3. Correction up creates the B-Point — must hold below Zero Point
4. Sequence activates only when price breaks below the A-Point
5. The TCL Short Zone is measured with Fibonacci Retracement from Zero to A
6. The extension target is projected with Fibonacci Extension from Zero → A → B

### Sequence Rule

- Never draw a sequence before activation
- Never assume an A-Point break before it actually happens
- If the A-Point is not broken, there is no active sequence and no trade

## Confluence Framework

Only use 3 primary confluence groups for decision-making:

### Primary Confluences (required)

1. Active sequence structure
2. Fibonacci entry level inside the TCL zone
3. Regime alignment (Weekly + Daily + 4H)

### Secondary Confluences (optional confirmation)

- Liquidation cluster inside the entry zone
- Weekly or Daily key level
- Point of Control (Volume Profile)
- 50 EMA or 200 EMA on 4H
- Wick fill / imbalance retest

### Setup Quality

- A+ Setup = Primary Confluences + at least 2 strong secondary confluences
- Standard Setup = Primary Confluences + at least 1 meaningful secondary confluence

## Market Mode Rules

- WAIT MODE: If ATR(14) is below its 30-bar baseline, ADX(14) is below 20, or the 24h moving average is flat — do not open breakout or sequence trades
- BREAKOUT MODE: If ATR(14) is above its 30-bar baseline, volume is above the 24h average, and price breaks the last 24h range — breakout trades are allowed
- SEQUENCE MODE: If ADX(14) is above 25, 4H structure confirms direction, and an active sequence exists — pullback entries at Fibonacci levels are allowed
- If 2 or more risk-off signals appear together — reduce size by 50% and only take A+ setups
- If 3 or more risk-off signals appear together — switch fully to WAIT MODE

## Regime Filter

Check Weekly, Daily, and 4H before every trade — this is mandatory.

### Bullish Inputs

- Weekly structure bullish (HH / HL)
- Daily close above 50-day moving average
- 4H structure bullish (HH / HL intact)

### Bearish Inputs

- Weekly structure bearish (LH / LL)
- Daily close below 50-day moving average
- 4H structure bearish (LH / LL intact)

### Regime Score

- 3x bullish = Long A+ setup allowed
- 2x bullish = Long standard setup allowed
- 1x bullish or less = WAIT, no long trade
- 3x bearish = Short A+ setup allowed
- 2x bearish = Short standard setup allowed
- 1x bearish or less = WAIT, no short trade

Do not trade Long in a bearish Weekly regime.
Do not trade Short in a bullish Weekly regime.

## Entry Rules

### Long — Sequence Entry

- Regime minimum 2x bullish
- Active bullish sequence present
- A-Point confirmed broken
- Price has entered the TCL Buy Zone (50.0% to 66.7%)
- At least 1 additional confluence factor present
- Enter level by level within the zone — not with one oversized order
- Preferred order of levels: 50.0% → 55.9% → 61.8% → 66.7%

### Long — Trigger Confirmation

Enter only when price is in the level and one of the following objective triggers appears on 1H or 15m:

- bullish engulfing candle
- rejection wick with close back above the level
- local structure break upward after touch
- volume spike in favor of the reversal

If no trigger appears, no entry.

### Long — Breakout Entry

- Regime minimum 2x bullish
- BREAKOUT MODE active
- Close above the highest high of the last 24 bars
- ATR expansion confirmed
- Volume above 24h average
- Active bullish sequence confirms breakout direction

### Short — Sequence Entry

- Regime minimum 2x bearish
- Active bearish sequence present
- A-Point confirmed broken
- Price has entered the TCL Short Zone (50.0% to 66.7%)
- At least 1 additional confluence factor present
- Enter level by level within the zone: 50.0% → 55.9% → 61.8% → 66.7%

### Short — Trigger Confirmation

Enter only when price is in the level and one of the following objective triggers appears on 1H or 15m:

- bearish engulfing candle
- rejection wick with close back below the level
- local structure break downward after touch
- volume spike in favor of the reversal

If no trigger appears, no entry.

### Short — Breakdown Entry

- Regime minimum 2x bearish
- BREAKOUT MODE active
- Close below the lowest low of the last 24 bars
- ATR expansion confirmed
- Volume above 24h average

### General Entry Filters

- Do not enter if price is already more than 2 ATR away from the 24h moving average
- Do not enter if no active sequence exists
- Do not draw sequences speculatively
- Minimum CRV must be 3:1 before any entry is taken

## Exit Rules

### Stop Loss

- Place the stop loss just beyond the 78.6% retracement of the active sequence
- If trading individual levels, the stop can be placed just beyond the next level for tighter risk
- Stop loss must always be placed immediately after entry

### Break-Even Rule

- Move stop to break-even only after price has moved 2x the initial stop-loss distance in your favor
- Never move stop earlier

### Take Profit 1

- Take partial profit when price has moved 3x the initial stop-loss distance in your favor
- Close 25% to 50% of the position

### Take Profit 2

- Close an additional 25% at the 161.8% Fibonacci Extension

### Take Profit 3

- Close an additional 25% at the 180.9% Fibonacci Extension

### Runner

- Keep the remaining position open for the 200.0% Fibonacci Extension
- Use a 2x ATR trailing stop or exit on 4H structure break, whichever happens first

### Time Stop

- If the position has not made meaningful progress after 48 hours, close it at market

## Position Sizing

- Risk amount = 1.5% of current equity for standard setups / 2.0% for A+ setups
- If trading multiple levels within a zone, split total risk across all planned entries
- Example: 1.5% total zone risk across 4 levels = 0.375% risk per level
- Position size = Risk amount ÷ (Entry price × Stop loss %)
- Apply leverage after calculating position size
- Max leverage: 3x for Longs / 2x for Shorts
- If required leverage exceeds the maximum, reduce position size until it fits
- Never exceed the risk limits regardless of conviction

## Drawdown Early Warning

Treat the market as risk-off if 2 or more of the following are true:

- ATR(14) falls below its 30-bar baseline
- ADX(14) falls below 20
- 24h moving average slope flattens sharply
- A breakout fails within 6 bars
- Two consecutive stopped trades occur in the same mode

In risk-off mode:

- Reduce position size by 50%
- Only take A+ setups
- No re-entry in the same direction for 12 hours unless a fresh breakout appears

If 3 or more are true:

- Switch completely to WAIT MODE

## A+ Setup Definition

An A+ setup requires all of the following:

- 3x regime score
- Active confirmed sequence
- Entry level inside the TCL zone
- At least 2 strong secondary confluences
- ATR expansion at entry
- Volume confirms the move
- CRV at least 3:1
- Market is not in WAIT MODE
- Daily and total drawdown limits are not hit

## What NOT To Do

- Don't trade in WAIT MODE
- Don't chase overextended candles
- Don't average down
- Don't move stop to break-even before the 2x SL-distance threshold
- Don't draw sequences before activation
- Don't overtrade — “no setup” is a valid decision
- Don't re-enter immediately after a stop loss unless a completely new signal appears
- Don't force Longs in a bearish Weekly regime
- Don't force Shorts in a bullish Weekly regime
- Don't revenge trade after losses
- Don't use secondary indicators as primary signals — sequence structure and Fibonacci levels come first
- Don't take any trade with CRV below 3:1
